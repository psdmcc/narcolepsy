DIET
============

Basically, I avoid carbs and simple sugars like the plague. Reducing/eliminating carbs in the morning and at lunch are a game-changer. 

Through trial and error, at least for me, the best thing that seems to work is have a protein rich breakfast within 30 mins of waking + 1 coffee with milk. I generally have 4 eggs and/or some meat leftover from the night before. If I have no meat/eggs then I'll have a protein shake. 

I generally have no limit to the amount of butter (not margarine or seed oils) that I consume. I also consume a lot of coconut oil. Typically putting them in my coffee. I think people have generally been scared to not eat 'fats' or 'salt.' Even though it seems we should be eating a lot more. Same for eggs. Our brains are mostly cholesterol, and all the good oils protect the nerves. It's a no brainer.

Unless I'm out and about being very active, or have gone to a social event that is centered around eating lunch, I will typically skip lunch all together. 

I generally eat a bunch of whatever food for dinner, not really holding back on what I eat, or necessarily how much. So, I do eat carbs at dinner. Though, I tend toward eating more meat and veggies/salads. Like the Vietnamese and Korean style of wrapping meat in lettuce. I think eating lots of good fresh veggies and lettuces is good.

I tend to eat between 5-6pm.

Some weeks, I might extend the intermittent fasting to 24-36 hours. 

Once I got used to not having lunch and avoiding carbs at breakfast, my energy levels went through the roof. I also lost a good 10-15kgs without a shred of extra exercise. 

I drink plenty of water.

Some of the things I take above potentiate alcohol. So I don't seem to manage more than 2-3 drinks. I don't really like being drunk anyway.

Every few months, I will do a parasite cleanse. I get some wormwood, black walnut and clove tincture and run that through till its gone.
