I'm not a medical doctor. This is not medical advice. 
I was diagnosed with chronic fatigue syndrome around 2002. I was later diagnosed with Type 1 narcolepsy in 2017. 
I was put on modafinil 400mg/day. It was intense. It wore off quick. I went back to having the multiple sleep attacks every day. Falling asleep while walking and waking up in a bush. 
Just having to lay down on the sidewalk to nap. Falling asleep at dinner. Dropping things or not being able to move. Falling 
asleep mid-sentence...

I've spent the past 10 years trying to figure out a daily protocol of pills and potions, etc., to work on fixing the issue. 
It seems that narcolepsy is an auto-immune illness that results from inflammation of the hypothalamus. 
The protocols I have developed maximise reducing inflammation and protecting neural pathways.

One of the biggest symptoms I used to experience was the feeling that deep inside my brain it was on fire. 
At some point, I thought to myself, "So is this inflammation? If 'yes', how to reduce it?"

This repository is not well organised. I will try to find the time to sort it out. 
There are peer-reviewed scientific articles on narcolepsy.
There are python scripts I used to scrape the NIH database and analyse the literature.
There is a very messy daily protocol file that has lots of information about narcolepsy and what 
the things I take do, how to avoid toxicity and having one thing cancel the other out. 

I've also set up a registry to log my daily consumptions and physical/mental states. You could replicate this. 

The protocol I use has enabled me to reduce my modafinil down to 100mg / day. 

I have all but deleted sleep attacks. 

This protocol includes stuff to take in the morning - to get going - and for the evening - to slow down.
