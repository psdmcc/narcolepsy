The backstory:

I was diagnosed with chronic fatigue syndrome around 2002. I was 23 yo, and essentially too sick to even get out of bed. While all my friends were off exploring the world, I was laying in bed...with no prospects of getting better.

IV chelation and a bunch of other stuff helped to rid the heavy metals in my body and repair it. I eventually got out of bed, but CFS continued to dominate my life. Wreaking havoc on professional and personal affairs.


I was later diagnosed with Type 1 narcolepsy in 2017. 

I was put on modafinil 400mg/day. It was intense. It wore off quick. I went back to having even worse symptoms. The multiple sleep attacks plagued me every day. I haad to have hourly naps. I'd fall asleep while walking, waking up in a bush.

Just having to lay down on the sidewalk to nap. Falling asleep at dinner. Dropping things or not being able to move. Falling 
asleep mid-sentence...


I've spent the past 10 years trying to figure out a daily protocol of pills and potions, etc., to work on fixing the issue. 

It seems that narcolepsy is an auto-immune illness that results from inflammation of the hypothalamus. 

The protocols I have developed maximise reducing inflammation and protecting neural pathways.

One of the biggest symptoms I used to experience was the feeling that deep inside my brain it was on fire. 
At some point, I thought to myself, "So is this inflammation? If 'yes', how to reduce it?"
